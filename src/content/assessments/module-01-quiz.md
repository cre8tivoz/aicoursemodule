# Module 01 — Assessment

**Purpose:** Summative evidence for **CO-01** (analyse trade-offs) and **CO-04** (creative direction with tools). **Judgement:** Satisfactory (**S**) / Not Satisfactory (**NS**) only—no percentage grade.

**Time guide:** 45–60 minutes. Submit in your provider’s format (document, LMS text box, or PDF).

## Assessment tasks

### Task 1 — Scenario: AI-assisted vs hand-authored (CO-01)

**Scenario:** A colleague wants a **three-page static site** (home, about, contact) for a side business. They can spend **one weekend** on it and may use **any** AI tool their workplace allows.

In **200–300 words**, **analyse** trade-offs of **AI-assisted** versus **mostly hand-authored** HTML/CSS for this situation. Address **at least three** of: speed, quality, maintainability, ethics (e.g. accuracy, disclosure, data policy). State a **clear recommendation** for this colleague and **why**, in plain language.

### Task 2 — Creative direction vs mechanical typing (CO-04)

In **4–6 sentences**, **contrast** **creative direction** (goals, audience, constraints) with **mechanical typing** of code. Give **one** example of a **creative-direction** decision that is **not** the same as “writing more lines of HTML.”

### Task 3 — When fundamentals still matter (CO-01)

List **three distinct situations** where **basic HTML/CSS literacy** still changes outcomes—even when AI drafts the first version. **One sentence each** (no paragraph required).

## Marking guide (Satisfactory / Not Satisfactory)

| Criterion | Satisfactory (S) | Not Satisfactory (NS) |
| --------- | ---------------- | ----------------------- |
| **Task 1 — Trade-off analysis** | Discusses **≥3** relevant factors; recommendation is **consistent** with the analysis; writing is understandable at ~Year 10 level. | Fewer than three factors, or no clear recommendation, or mostly generic claims without application to the scenario. |
| **Task 1 — AQF depth** | Shows **judgement** (weighs pros/cons), not only bullet lists of facts. | Only lists tool names or repeats course slogans without analysis. |
| **Task 2 — Contrast** | Clearly separates **direction** from **typing**; example is **specific** and plausible. | Confuses the two ideas, or example is missing or irrelevant. |
| **Task 3 — Situations** | Three **distinct**, **plausible** situations (e.g. accessibility check, fixing structure, better prompts). | Fewer than three, or duplicates, or vague (“when things go wrong”). |

**Overall module judgement:** **S** only if **all** rows are **S** at an acceptable Diploma-level standard. **NS** if any row is **NS**.

## What good looks like (examples)

These are **illustrative** — write in your own words. Do not copy verbatim.

**Task 1 (extract):**

> For a weekend side-business site, AI-assisted work wins on speed for first drafts — I could have three linked pages live by Sunday afternoon. The trade-off is verification time: I would still check heading order, contact details, and workplace AI policy before publish. Hand-coding would teach me more about maintenance but might not finish in one weekend. **Recommendation:** use AI inside the IDE for structure and copy drafts, then read every file before deploy — speed with oversight.

**Task 2:**

> Creative direction is deciding *what* the site must achieve and for whom; mechanical typing is entering tags. Choosing “one `h1` for the business name and contact in the footer on every page” is direction — it is not the same as pasting 200 lines the model returned.

**Task 3 (three situations):**

1. Fixing skipped heading levels before publish.  
2. Catching a wrong phone number the model invented.  
3. Writing a tighter prompt because you know what `<nav>` should contain.

## Exemplar responses (for assessors)

**Task 1 (shape only):** Look for named trade-offs (e.g. speed vs verification time), mention of hallucination or policy, and a reasoned pick—not “AI always wins” or “never use AI” without nuance.

**Task 2:** Accept definitions in learner’s words; example might be “choose one `h1` and section order” vs “paste whatever the model outputs.”

**Task 3:** Accept e.g. heading order for screen readers, contrast/CSS review, debugging broken tags, writing precise prompts.
