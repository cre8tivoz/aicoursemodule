# Capstone alignment: what you will submit

Module 06 turns **intent** (brief), **execution** (prompts and loops), and **judgement** (review) into something **assessable**: your **capstone** static mini-site. This lesson lines up your work so far with the **exact** requirements in [`assessments/capstone.md`](../../assessments/capstone.md). Nothing here adds a new hurdle—it **clarifies** what “done” means for submission.

## Introduction

The capstone is **1–3 HTML pages**, linked together, **deployed** publicly, with **Lighthouse Accessibility ≥ 90%** on the **deployed** URL. You hand-author **HTML** and **CSS**; frameworks are **not** required. The course site uses Eleventy for **these** lessons; **your** capstone does **not** have to use Eleventy—use plain files if you prefer.

Your **brief** (Lesson 01) should already name pages and purpose. This lesson asks you to **trace** each brief section to a **deliverable** and a **piece of evidence** an assessor can see.

## Map brief → pages

| Brief element | Capstone mapping |
| ------------- | ---------------- |
| **Goal** | Clear purpose on home page; visitor understands “what this is” in one scroll. |
| **Audience** | Readable typography, mobile layout, tone in copy. |
| **Pages/sections** | 1–3 HTML documents; internal nav works on all. |
| **Must-haves** | Each becomes a row in your self-audit (see practice). |
| **Accessibility intent** | Semantic HTML, keyboard use, visible focus, contrast; **Lighthouse** score recorded. |
| **Non-goals** | Stay disciplined—extra scope risks broken layout or missed checks. |

If your brief promised five pages, **narrow** the brief before submission—the capstone allows **1–3** pages only.

## Deployment and CO-05

You must publish a **live URL**. Common options (not an exhaustive endorsement list):

- **Netlify**, **Vercel**, or similar **static** hosts—drag-and-drop or Git-based deploys.
- **Your own server** with HTTPS preferred.

**Justify** your choice in context: cost, privacy, familiarity, organisational IT rules. A two-sentence note in your submission is enough: e.g. “Hosted on Netlify for free tier and HTTPS; no backend needed for static files.”

## Evidence pack (what assessors expect)

Minimum:

1. **URL** — loads without login; assessor can open it.  
2. **Lighthouse** — **Accessibility** score **≥ 90%** on the **deployed** site. Save a **screenshot** or write the **score, date, and Chrome version** if your provider asks for offline evidence. Remember: Lighthouse **supports** assessment; it is **not** a legal compliance certificate (as stated in the capstone doc).

Optional but helpful:

- Link to **source** (GitHub, GitLab, zip)—proves authorship if questioned.  
- Short **changelog** of what you asked AI vs edited by hand—**honesty**, not shame.

## Marking: S / NS in plain language

The capstone uses **Satisfactory** / **Not Satisfactory** per row in the marking guide. **Overall S** requires **every** row at **S** level. If accessibility falls below threshold or the site is unreachable, the outcome is **NS** regardless of good intentions.

Rows cover: **structure/UX**, **accessibility**, **visual design**, **deployment**, **content** (original, appropriate). Read the full table in [`assessments/capstone.md`](../../assessments/capstone.md)—this lesson does not replace it.

## Linking prompts and review to the rows

- **Structure / UX** — Your **brief** + **Lesson 02** prompts should demand nav, hierarchy, and mobile behaviour; **Lesson 03** loop catches regressions.  
- **Accessibility** — Module 03 checklist + Module 05 literacy + Lighthouse on **deployed** URL.  
- **Visual design** — Intentional palette from your brief; avoid “random” colours that fail contrast.  
- **Deployment** — CO-05: working URL.  
- **Content** — Your words; respect copyright on images and text.

### Common pitfalls before you submit

**Local vs deployed Lighthouse:** Run Lighthouse on the **live** URL. Scores can differ from `file://` opens because of paths, fonts, or caching. If your score is borderline, fix **contrast** and **focus** first—they are frequent drag factors.

**Broken relative links after deploy:** A link that works on your laptop might break if folder structure changes on upload. Click **every** nav item on the deployed site in a **fresh** browser session.

**Placeholder content:** “Lorem ipsum” or obvious AI filler can look like **missing** authentic content under the **Content** row. Replace with real sentences—even simple ones—about your topic.

**Image rights:** Use your own photos, clearly licensed stock, or placeholders you are allowed to ship. Copied text or images without permission can fail **content** expectations and create legal risk outside this course.

### Timeboxing the final loop

Give yourself a **deadline** for “content freeze” (no new sections) and a separate block for **accessibility pass** only. Mixing new features with a11y fixes often causes **regressions**. The capstone rewards **complete** and **coherent**, not **feature-rich** and broken. Sleep helps too: a fresh read catches embarrassing typos and broken links.

## Key takeaways

- Capstone = **brief** + **1–3 pages** + **deploy** + **evidence** (Lighthouse accessibility).  
- **S** requires **all** rows; fix **weakest** row first.  
- Your **brief** and **prompts** show how you directed tools; your **URL** shows where you published.
