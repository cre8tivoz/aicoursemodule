# Creative director — in ten minutes

Small web projects usually fail on **intent**, not technology. Nobody agreed what "done" meant.

In this course you are the **creative director** — even as a team of one. Your IDE and AI tools are instruments. The brief is the score.

[Module 06, Lesson 01](/lessons/module-06/01) walks you through the **full one-page brief** for your capstone. Here you learn the **idea** in one sitting.

## The one sentence to keep

**Tools amplify direction.** Clear direction produces useful output faster. Vague direction produces confident nonsense faster.

## What a brief answers (minimum)

- **Goal** — What outcome should the page support?
- **Audience** — Who visits, and what do they already know?
- **Scope** — How many pages? What is in and out?
- **Tone** — Pick words a teammate would recognise (friendly, formal, plain…).
- **Definition of done** — What would you check before saying "ship"?

**Acceptance criteria** turn "looks good" into checks you can run: one `<h1>`, contact visible on a phone, images have alt text. You will refine these in later modules.

## Vague vs useful (30 seconds)

**Weak:** "Make a website for my hobby."

**Stronger:**

> **Goal:** Help neighbours book a 1-hour intro for a community garden club.  
> **Audience:** Adults in my suburb; many use phones.  
> **Scope:** One scrolling page — intro, meeting times, map link, email. No login.  
> **Done means:** A new visitor finds when and where within 30 seconds on a phone.

Paste the stronger version into an AI tool or hand it to a designer. Same task, less random rework.

## Do this — five-bullet sketch (10 minutes)

Draft a **five-bullet** brief for a fictional micro-site (hobby you do not do, or a dream landing page):

1. Audience  
2. Scope  
3. Tone  
4. Must-haves  
5. Definition of done  

Read it aloud. If you stumble, your visitor might too.

Keep this draft — you will expand it into the full template in [Module 06](/lessons/module-06/01).
