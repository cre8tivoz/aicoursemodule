# freeCodeCamp and other free foundations (optional depth)

This course teaches **AI-assisted static sites**, not a full web-dev degree. To deepen **reading** skills, many learners use **[freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/)** or peers like **MDN Learning Area** — **optional**, **self-paced**, **not a hurdle**.

Completing certifications here is **not required** to pass this course or the capstone.

## What to open first

| If your gap is… | Start with… |
| --------------- | ----------- |
| Cannot read AI HTML | Responsive Web Design — markup modules |
| CSS confuses you | Basic CSS before Grid mastery |
| Light interactivity later | JavaScript track (not required for capstone) |

**Healthy AI pattern:** try 10 minutes yourself → ask for a **hint**, not the full answer → **type** the fix so your fingers learn.

## Do this — 30-minute trial (today or this week)

1. Open [freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/) **or** one alternative you will actually use.  
2. Pick **one** section for your next study block.  
3. Write a **one-sentence** goal (specific — not “learn everything”).  

After **30 minutes**, three lines:

- What I did: ___  
- What clicked: ___  
- Next time I will: ___  

**Honesty check (two sentences):** Why might you still use AI after 100 hours of tutorials? (Speed is legitimate — **verification** stays yours.)

### Why fundamentals and AI work together

Studying basics while using AI is **strategy**, not contradiction. You decide where human attention goes: prompts, structure, review. Literacy helps you **steer** output instead of being steered by it.

## If freeCodeCamp is not your taste

Switch resources without guilt. What matters is **steady contact** with HTML/CSS until reading feels less scary — paper cheat sheets and offline MDN count.
